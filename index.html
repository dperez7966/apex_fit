How To Test Safely

Do not attempt a true 1-rep max. Instead, find a weight you can lift for 5 clean reps with good form — your last rep should be genuinely challenging. We calculate your estimated 1RM using the Epley formula. Always warm up with 2-3 progressively heavier sets first. Rest 3-4 minutes between exercises.

How To Test

The 1.5 Mile Run is the standard cardiovascular fitness test. Use a flat track or treadmill. Cover 1.5 miles as fast as you can sustain and record your time. If you are not yet able to run continuously, walk briskly and record your honest time — that is your baseline.

How To Test

Perform each test fresh, resting 2 minutes between exercises. Push-ups: full reps to failure, no sagging hips. Plank: forearm hold until form breaks. Sit-ups: max reps in 60 seconds. These three numbers will change significantly within 8 weeks of consistent training.

How To Test

Rate each screen honestly on a 1-3 scale. 1 = significant restriction, 2 = achievable with compensation, 3 = clean movement. These screens identify movement limitations that increase injury risk in your strength training — addressing them directly will improve your lifts.
